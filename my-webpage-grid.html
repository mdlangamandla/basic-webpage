Historic places in Cape Town

District Six Museum

The area was named in 1867 as the Sixth Municipal District of Cape Town. The area began to grow after the freeing of the enslaved in 1833.In the 1940s plans were formed by the Cape Town municipality to demolish houses under slum clearance, but it was only after the declaration of District Six as a white area under the Group Areas Act in 1966 that extensive demolition began.

City Hall

City Hall have a statue of Nelson Mandela on the balcony overlooking the Grand Parade and the statue is on the same spot where he made a speech when he was released from prison on 11 February 1990. The tower of the City Hall has a Turret Clock which strikes the hours and chimes the Westminster quarters and the clock has a 24-hour wheel and lever

Castle of Good Hope

Castle of Good Hope was build between 1666 and 1679, the Castle is the oldest existing building in South Africa.The Castle of Good Hope operated as the seat of government of the DEIC until 1795, when the colony was occupied by the British.When you visit the Castle of Good Hope you can also catch the changing of the guard at noon and the Key Ceremony (replicating the ceremonial unlocking of the Castle) at 10am and noon every weekday.
